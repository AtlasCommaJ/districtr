Redistricting is a long and consequential process that starts every ten years after the Census data is released. Where and how the lines are drawn influences everything from who can get elected to how federal, state, and local resources get allocated.

And communities matter. The process is different in every state—some states collect public maps and communities of interest (COIs). Community members can participate in the redistricting process by submitting public testimony. Our COI explainer talks more about this.
